Decorations 🏺
When it comes to placing decorations, it's important to notice that sometimes they can be clutters too! All the clutter in our home could mirror the overwhelm, stress, and hectic busyness of our everyday lives.
Lighting 💡
Lighting is curcial in effecting your overall energy (qi) and your Feng Shui! Be sure to utilize moderate lighting in your livingroom and avoid dim or over-bright lighting.
Door/ Room Entrance 🚪
While it could be hard to control your livingroom entrance, it's important to notice that "seeing the livingroom's commanding position from the room's entrance" is not the most ideal Feng Shui.

Because the door represents the flow of energy, it is best to avoid this situation.

Couch/ Sofa 🛋
The couch is usually most suitable to be placed against a wall or any concrete backings, as they are often dominating the commanding positon in the living room.

By placing the couch against a wall symbolizes a supporting system, which can help the household to maintain good luck.

Wall Art 🖼
While art is completely based on personal preference, it is important to be mindful of the following three points: color, associated emotions, and height.

First, it you can bring in meaningful colors or the five elements through an image. For example, a fiery red painting invokes fire energy for passion, recognition, and visibility.

Second, if possible, avoid art that is emotionally charged or that has a history with you.

Thirdly, avoid hanging the art too low, as the art can associate with low energy.

Plants 🪴
Living green plants bring life energy into your home. It’s a super simple way to bring good Feng Shui into your living room. Be sure to provide enough light, space, water, and TLC.
Windows 🪟
It's important to allow air into your livingroom! This allows for any old and stale energy to leave and make room for new. And while you’re at it, clean the windows.
